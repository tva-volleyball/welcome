Our Mission

"To provide an affordable club experience for academically-focused high-schoolers who want to continue to develop their passion in volleyball and enhance their college application with a passion project."

Program Leadership & Training

Practice Schedule: 3 times per week

Location: Local middle school(s)

Team Structure

We do not form team(s) by age group. Instead, we will form either 1 or 2 teams based strictly on skill level. Teams compete in the 15-18s division for WCVBA leagues and events. For Far Western (NCVA), we may compete in the 16s or 17s age level depending on the oldest player’s birthday on the team.

Why Tri-Valley Alliances?

  • Academic Balance: We focus on academically oriented kids who want a club path that lets them balance school priorities with competitive volleyball.
  • Reduced Travel Time: Most players will be from local high schools in the immediate area, cutting down on commute times.
  • Minimized Costs & Disruptions: We play exclusively in the WCVBA league and do not chase multiple far-away qualifiers, protecting your weekends and avoiding missed school days.
  • Non-Profit, Transparent Structure: An extremely thin organization means significantly lower costs to players to relieve financial burdens. Every dollar is fully transparent.

The Leadership Incubator Framework

We do not behave like traditional clubs. Beyond volleyball on the court, we focus heavily on the off-the-court experience. While the club provides the operational infrastructure (NCVA/WCVBA membership, insurance, facilities), the players are responsible for the team's operational life.

This model deliberately shifts traditional "Team Mom" tasks into student-led initiatives, acting as a perfect passion project to strengthen athletes' college applications!

Player Task List (Off-the-Court Projects):

Brand & Aesthetic Design Designing the team logo, choosing unique team names, selecting color palettes, and creating custom jerseys/spirit wear.
Financial Management & Fundraising Drafting a team budget, tracking active expenses, organizing 'Serve-a-thons', or pitching to local business sponsors.
Logistics & Travel Planning Building tournament 'Go-Bag' checklists, managing packing schedules, and researching team dinners and bonding activities for the Reno Qualifier.
Communications & Marketing Managing the team’s social media footprints and distributing internal newsletters.
Event Planning & Gameday Ops Coordinating end-of-season celebrations, parent appreciation events, managing match day schedules, setting up recording equipment, and tracking officiating duties.
